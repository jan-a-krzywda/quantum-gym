# Specification: world_model_spec_high.md (Pure VAE / Free Energy Principle)

## Overview
This "High" architecture fully embraces the Free Energy Principle. It abandons sequential RNN tracking entirely in favor of a purely parallel Variational Autoencoder (VAE). Here, the Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO) explicitly serves as the Variational Free Energy bounding the agent's surprise.

## 1. Architecture details
* **Encoder (MLP):** A fast, parallel Multi-Layer Perceptron. Maps the concatenated basis $B$ and outcome $S$ into the variational posterior $q_{\phi}(z | S, B)$.
* **Latent Space ($z$):** Continuous representation of the hidden causes of the quantum state.
* **Decoder (MLP):** A parallel Multi-Layer Perceptron. Maps $(z, B)$ directly to the 17 independent Bernoulli probabilities for the bitstring $P_{\theta}(S | B, z)$ in a single forward pass. No sequential token-by-token generation.

## 2. Training Objective: Minimizing Variational Free Energy
The agent updates its beliefs by minimizing the Variational Free Energy (VFE), which is the negative ELBO:
$$VFE = D_{KL}(q_{\phi}(z | S, B) || p(z)) - \mathbb{E}_{q_{\phi}} [\log P_{\theta}(S | B, z)]$$
* This approach is blisteringly fast compared to the RNN, as both encoding and decoding are fully parallelized matrix multiplications.

## 3. Action Phase: Expected Free Energy (EFE) via Latent Entropy
Because the decoder outputs independent probabilities in a single pass, EFE can be estimated differently.
* **Epistemic Uncertainty:** Mapped directly to the variance of the latent space $\sigma^2$ generated by the encoder for a specific basis.
* **Aleatoric Uncertainty:** The Shannon entropy of the decoder's output probabilities.
* The agent selects the basis $B$ that maximizes the combined entropy of the decoder and the latent spread, directly optimizing the classic Active Inference EFE functional without needing to manually calculate physical stabilizer parities.
